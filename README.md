# The Impact of Physical Fatigue on Gait Metrics

**Honors Thesis** · UNC Wilmington · Department of Psychology · 2024

![SPSS](https://img.shields.io/badge/SPSS-analysis-blue) ![Jamovi](https://img.shields.io/badge/Jamovi-stats-blue) ![SportGait BKG](https://img.shields.io/badge/SportGait-BKG-green)

---

## Overview

This study investigates how physical fatigue affects gait dynamics, specifically speed, power, balance, and symmetry  using the SportGait BioKinetoGraph (BKG), a mobile accelerometer-based application. Using a within-group experimental design, gait was measured before and after participants completed their usual workout at the UNCW Recreation Center. Fatigue was assessed through self-reported measures (Multidimensional Fatigue Inventory) and heart rate (Garmin watch).

---

## Key Findings

- **Self-reported fatigue increased significantly post-workout** (Cohen's d = 7.34), while heart rate did not change significantly — suggesting these two measures capture different dimensions of fatigue.
- **Gait changes were small but domain-specific.** Significant increases in swing phase and stride time indicate participants walked slower after exercise. Vertical power decreased significantly, suggesting reduced force generation.
- **Stability and symmetry showed trends but not significance** — side sway and forward movement symmetry approached significance, likely limited by sample size.
- **SR fatigue and HR were not correlated**, reinforcing that subjective and physiological fatigue are distinct constructs.

---

## Methods

**Participants:** 69 adults (ages 18–64) recruited from the UNCW Recreation Center. 52 completed post-workout measures.

**Design:** Within-group, pre/post experimental design. Participants completed their self-selected workout between assessments.

**Tools:** SportGait BioKinetoGraph (BKG) · Multidimensional Fatigue Inventory (MFI) · Garmin heart rate monitor · SPSS · Jamovi

---

## Why It Matters

Current concussion assessments often overlook the confounding effects of physical fatigue, which can mimic concussion symptoms and lead to misdiagnosis or premature return-to-play decisions. This research works toward establishing a fatigue-specific gait signature that can be distinguished from concussion-related gait patterns — with implications for clinical and athletic settings.
